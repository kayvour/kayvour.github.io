I Left the Light On

I Left the Light On

I left the light on Just to see what the dark would do. The light hits me in the face, As if it knows something beautiful. My feet are cold from the tiled floor, Light clinging on like leeches. How do I test these boundaries If there aren't any around me? Do I build a brick wall, To keep selective memories Of you, and me with you? Or should I not think so far? Don't close my eyes, I think. That's not real enough to feel it. I need the dark to reach out, To validate my dreaming.
